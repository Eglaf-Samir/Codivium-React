Your Scores, Explained

Codivium measures what you can actually do — not hours logged. Here's what each score represents and what genuinely moves it.

Master score

Codivium Score  0 – 100

Combines your breadth (how widely you can apply skills) and your depth (how reliably you apply them) into a single mastery score. Both need to be strong — neither compensates for a very low score in the other. It reflects well-rounded, consistent competency rather than expertise concentrated in one area.

A very low breadth or depth score constrains the overall — both pillars matter

The two pillars
🗺️
Breadth

How widely you can apply your skills

What fraction of the category catalog you've meaningfully engaged with, across Micro Challenges, Interview Preparation, and MCQ. Evenly distributed practice scores higher than the same hours concentrated in one area.

↑ First clean solve in a new category ↑ Balanced practice across categories ↓ Concentrating only on familiar areas
🎯
Depth

How reliably you solve on early attempts

Whether you tend to solve correctly on attempt 1 or 2, rather than requiring many retries. Has diminishing returns by design — the jump from "can't solve" to "sometimes solves" matters most. Fades slowly with inactivity.

↑ Solving on first or second attempt ↑ Consistent recent performance ↓ High attempt counts or long absence
Supporting metrics
🏆

Codivium Points Uncapped, all-time

Cumulative score weighted by difficulty and solve speed. Recent sessions count more than older ones. Re-doing the same exercise within 7 days adds no extra points — the system rewards new progress, not repetition. Shown alongside a 30-day window to reflect current momentum. Points are separate from your Codivium Score and represent your career trajectory on the platform.

⚡

Efficiency Points per hour

Quality solves produced per hour of active coding time. Cannot be inflated by spending more hours — only by improving the quality of output relative to time spent. A declining efficiency while hours increase is an early signal to change practice approach.

📊

Convergence Heatmap

Shows your pass rate on your 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, and 5th+ attempts per category. Each cell is the fraction of attempts in that bucket where you passed all unit tests. A strong heatmap shows high rates on early attempts across many categories — that's what your Depth score rewards.

📚

MCQ Performance

Average score per category at each difficulty level, accumulated across all your quiz sessions. Unlike coding exercises, MCQ doesn't use attempt-bucket tracking — your percentage score per quiz session is the direct input to the scoring model.

🕐

All scores use recency weighting. Recent sessions count more than older ones — your scores reflect where you are now, not where you were when you started. Depth fades gradually after extended inactivity in a category, with harder categories fading more slowly than easier ones.
